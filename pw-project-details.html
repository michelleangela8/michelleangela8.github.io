10.015 Physical World

Project Overview

Many less-developed countries (LDCs) struggle to afford advanced seismometers, leaving their populations vulnerable to earthquakes. Recognizing that preparedness significantly reduces casualties, we developed Earthquickie, a low-cost pendulum-based alarm system that provides early warnings during seismic events.

Solution

The prototype utilizes pendulum oscillations to detect earthquake vibrations. When seismic activity causes the pendulum to swing past a critical angle, it completes an electrical circuit via strategically placed magnets, triggering an alarm. This design ensures quick alerts, allowing people to seek shelter immediately.

Design Considerations

  • Multi-Pendulum System: Final design incorporated three pendulums instead of one to capture a wider range of frequencies, increasing accuracy.
  • Structural Evolution: The alarm's structure evolved from a rectangular box to a pyramid shape for better stability.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: Cost-effective materials like ice cream sticks and small metal plates were used to ensure affordability.

Impact

The prototype presents a scalable, cost-effective solution to earthquake preparedness in LDCs, potentially saving lives by providing timely warnings where expensive seismometers are not an option.
