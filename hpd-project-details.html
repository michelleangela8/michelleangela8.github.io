30.203 Healthcare Product Design

Project Overview

Dementia affects over 55 million people globally, leading to cognitive decline, memory loss, and reduced quality of life. Current treatments, both pharmacological and non-pharmacological, face limitations such as side effects, lack of personalization, and adoption barriers. There is a need for engaging, sensory-based interventions that can enhance cognitive well-being in people with dementia (PLWD).

Solution

Our team developed Khaki Zhu, a cooking-based reminiscence therapy game that integrates sensor-driven biofeedback to trigger memories and enhance engagement in PLWD. The game simulates traditional Singaporean cuisine preparation, such as making bak chang, a nostalgic dish for many elderly Singaporeans. It uses:

  • Immersive market and cooking environments from 1960sâ€“1970s Singapore.
  • Real-time biofeedback sensors (EDA & Pulse Oximeter) to monitor player engagement and emotional responses.
  • Tailored to cultural backgrounds to evoke memories.
  • Automated diffuser activation in response to significant biofeedback changes, enhancing sensory stimulation.

Design Considerations

  • Comfort & Accessibility: Sensors must be lightweight (lighter than 150g), comfortable, and easy to set up (less than 1 minute).
  • Real-Time Data Processing: A data refresh rate of 60 Hz, with readings updated every 0.05 sec for immediate response.
  • Familiarity & Nostalgia: Designed with traditional kitchen tools, market environments, and cultural references to evoke memories.

Impact

This sensor-driven reminiscence therapy game provides an interactive, low-barrier intervention for dementia care, helping enhance cognitive stimulation and emotional well-being through culturally familiar experiences. The integration of real-time biofeedback and sensory stimulation makes it a personalized and engaging therapy tool for PLWD and caregivers.
