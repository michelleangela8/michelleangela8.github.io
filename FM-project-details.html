30.103 Fluid Mechanics

Project Overview

Preclinical drug testing is essential to evaluate the bioactivity, safety, and efficacy of newly formulated drugs. Traditional methods rely on bulky equipment and expensive in vivo (animal-based) experiments, which are not always reliable. There is a need for a more cost-effective, scalable, and flexible alternative for drug screening.

Solution

Our team developed a modular microfluidic system to generate controlled drug dosage gradients for cell-based screening. The system consists of interconnected microfluidic chips that enable precise mixing and dilution of drug solutions. The modular design allows researchers to customize experimental setups by adding or removing specific microfluidic components.

Design Considerations

  • Scalability: The system enables researchers to adjust dilution levels by modifying the number of mixing channels.
  • Reusability: Components can be sanitized and reused for different drug tests, reducing waste.
  • Ease of Fabrication: Components were manufactured using 3D printing (SLA & FDM), and PDMS casting, ensuring rapid prototyping and flexibility.

Impact

This modular, low-cost microfluidic system enhances drug screening efficiency by reducing reliance on expensive equipment and animal testing. It provides a scalable, reusable, and high-precision alternative for biomedical research, supporting the development of safer and more effective pharmaceuticals.
