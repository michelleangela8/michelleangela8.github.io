Capstone Project

Project Overview

Indoor CO₂ buildup can harm health and reduce cognitive performance, especially in enclosed or poorly ventilated spaces. To tackle this, we developed a smart IoT ventilation system that monitors CO₂ levels with an SCD40 sensor and automatically controls a fan via ESP32 modules. The system ensures healthier air quality with real-time monitoring, energy-efficient fan control, and remote access through ESP RainMaker.

Solution

An IoT-enabled CO₂ monitoring and ventilation system that tracks indoor air quality in real time and automatically controls a fan to maintain safe levels. Our solution has these key features:

  • Fast local communication using ESP-NOW
  • Accurate sensing with the SCD40 sensor
  • Remote monitoring and manual override via the ESP RainMaker mobile app

Benefits

  • Improved Safety: Reduces heat-related injuries with early intervention. Tested gait detected accuracy of 88.52%
  • Lightweight & Comfortable: Designed for an off-body attachment at the waist, considered the most stable placement for an IMU at the torso. Does not adhere to skin directly.
  • Dual Charging Mode: Available at two different versions, a wireless charging version compatible with a pouch and a cable charging version with a metal belt attachment.
  • Easy Integration: Seamless Bluetooth connection to a user-friendly app.
  • Cloud Dashboard: Allows for central monitoring of multiple workers.

Impact

Gait analysis will allow for early detection of fatigue, reducing the risk of heat-related injuries. This solution is particularly beneficial for workers in high-risk environments, such as construction sites and factories, where heat stress is a significant concern. By providing real-time data and analytics, our solution empowers employers to take proactive measures to ensure the safety and well-being of their workforce.
