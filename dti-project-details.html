03.007 Design Thinking and Innovation

Project Overview

The area outside the Expo MRT is underutilized due to extreme weather conditions, lack of shelter, and an absence of inviting public seating. Passersby, families, and nearby office workers lack a comfortable space for social interactions. The project aims to enhance the functionality of the space by incorporating innovative structures that provide shade, seating, and better ventilation.

Solution

The Orbuculum is an outdoor seating and shelter system designed to improve public spaces. The structure features a curved dome with sliding panels, offering both shade and ventilation. The dome utilizes a motorized step mechanism controlled by temperature sensors, ensuring automatic adjustment based on weather conditions. The seating incorporates ergonomic design improvements, including curved edges for safety and added railings for accessibility. The final prototype includes integrated lighting and projection features, enhancing nighttime usability and promoting social engagement.

Design Considerations

  • Weather Adaptability: Retractable railings and sliding panels adjust to temperature changes.
  • Aesthetic & Functional Appeal: CAD-optimized dome design encourages public interaction.
  • Sustainability: Durable materials selected for long-term outdoor use.

Tools & Technologies Used

  • CAD Software: Used SOLIDWORKS for modeling the dome, seating, and elevation structures.
  • Rendering: Used Blender to render key images for the project.
  • Prototyping Materials: Acrylic, aluminum, and motorized components for automated adjustments.

Impact

This project showcases design thinking, human-centered prototyping, and automation, creating a more engaging and functional urban space.
